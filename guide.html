A Guide to Delhi: The National Capital

Welcome to Delhi

Delhi, India's vibrant capital, offers a mesmerizing blend of history and modernity. Start your journey in Old Delhi, where the narrow lanes tell stories from the Mughal era. Visit the majestic Red Fort, the grand Jama Masjid, and dive into the chaos of Chandni Chowk, known for its street food and traditional markets. The scent of freshly fried samosas and sweet jalebis will tempt your taste buds while rickshaws zip past you in a whirl of color and sound.

New Delhi Highlights

Next, head to New Delhi, designed by British architect Edwin Lutyens. This part of the city boasts wide boulevards and colonial-era buildings. Don't miss India Gate, a war memorial surrounded by lush gardens, and Rashtrapati Bhavan, the official residence of the President. The contrast between New Delhi's planned elegance and Old Delhi’s lived-in chaos gives the city a unique duality that fascinates every visitor.

Cultural Cityscape

For a cultural and spiritual experience, explore landmarks like Lotus Temple, Humayun’s Tomb, and the towering Qutub Minar. Delhi’s museums, vibrant bazaars, and diverse culinary scene—from five-star dining to street stalls—reflect the city’s dynamic spirit. Whether you're sipping chai in a quiet park or bargaining in a bustling bazaar, Delhi promises an unforgettable experience with every step.
